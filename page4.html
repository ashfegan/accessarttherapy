What is Art Therapy?

Art therapy is a form of psychotherapy that uses creative practices like drawing, painting, collage and sculpture to explore emotions and concepts. Aisling offers art therapy that is trauma informed. It suitable for people who have experienced trauma, domestic and political violence, injustice, eating problems, abuse, low mood, isolation, worry, grief and loneliness.

Some thoughts and feelings can be very difficult to put into words. Art therapy offers an alternative way to safely express complex feelings. You can communicate at your own pace; with or without words.

Art therapy as a form of social action works to create change, elevate awareness of social problems, provide community service, understand the origins of differing social behaviour, offer instruction in socially oriented action and increase sensitivity to the social context of human suffering. (Kaplan 2015)

You do not need to have any particular skill in art to make use of the art therapy.  You do not even have to make art in sessions! It is not dependant on spoken language so it is suitable for all ages.
Any artwork made is confidential. 

Group Therapy

Aisling offers long-term, art therapy groups; underpinned using psychodynamic and group analytic thinking.  There is a maximum of eight people; meeting at the same time very week for 90 minutes; either online or in-person in Newark-on-Trent.  Group therapy can help you to find your voice, express difficult thoughts and feeling, establish and maintain healthy relationships, improve communication skills, reduce feelings of isolation and make sense of how the past influences the present.


How does it work?
Groups are a microcosm of our wider society. So, dynamics arising in group therapy echo our wider social, cultural and political context. Group therapy offers an opportunity to think beyond our social norms. This is something one of the founding members of the Institute of Group Analysis, Patrick De Maré referred to as outsight; “the outward expansion of social consciousness and thoughtfulness”. (Lenn & Stefano, 2012, p. 129) This can be a life line for people who are suffering because of social or cultural circumstances.

About Aisling

Pronouns: she / her
Irish Gaelic name pronounced: Ash-ling.

Aisling Fegan is an Irish artist and community art therapist (HCPC registered) based in Newark-on-Trent. She graduated from Goldsmiths University of London in 2015 with a masters in art psychotherapy, has a degree in modelmaking and design for film and theatre from Ireland’s National Film School (2007) and a diploma in group analytic psychotherapy with Group Analysis India (2022).

She is a lived experience practitioner. So, she does not see through a medicalised lens. Both her practice as a multimedia artist and work as an art therapist deeply resonate with geo-historical, social, environmental, political and cultural issues. Aisling offers art therapy and reflective practice sessions for people living locally in Newark & internationally online. She proudly works for icap; an organisation that supports members of the Irish community in Britain.
