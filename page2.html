ABOUT

We do not need to be isolated in our isolation

Access Art Therapy offers confidential art psychotherapy for people living within reach of Croydon. This form of psychoanalysis can be helpful for people who are suffering because of trauma, bereavement, stress, burnout, anxiety, depression, memory difficulties, displacement, the affects of illness, loss and separation. These are safe and non-judgemental spaces. Underpinned using psychodynamic and group analytic thinking, Access Art Therapy is a community based, private practice, conducted by Aisling Fegan, a HCPC registered art psychotherapist. Clinical supervision is also available for therapists and frontline workers. 


What is art therapy?

Art therapy, also known as art psychotherapy, is a type of psychological therapy as opposed to an art class or hobby. It offers an accessible way to communicate and contain thoughts and feelings, too difficult to express with words alone. You do not have to make art and there is no particular skill required to use art psychotherapy, therefore sessions are suitable for people of all ages and abilities. The artwork made and content of these sessions is confidential.

Art psychotherapy groups offer a dynamic environment that can enable people to gain deeper insight, maintain capacity to think and sustain the ability to reflect with others. Supporting people through the emotional impact of the COVID-19 pandemic, these groups can create a strengthening sense of togetherness. As an alternative to talking therapies, art psychotherapy offers a way to safely connect with others and share underlying issues.


Therapy sessions are currently held online. Early morning and evening sessions are available to accommodate shift patterns, parental responsibilities and care related commitments. An initial consultation is required, to learn more about your needs and think together about the benefits and how these sessions might be for you.

AISLING FEGAN

Known as Ash, I am a HCPC registered art psychotherapist, clinical supervisor, organisational consultant and Croydon resident; working with people of all ages and across a broad range of issues including trauma, grief, anxiety, abuse, addiction, dementia and domestic violence.

Between June 2017 and April 2020, I worked as a clinical lead in the immediate aftermath and community-led, crisis response to the Grenfell Tower fire. An experienced operations manager, I am also a former Senior Dementia Advisor for the Royal Borough of Kensington and Chelsea.

I hold a Masters in art psychotherapy, qualifying with distinctions from Goldsmiths University in 2015. I am currently undertaking further training at the Institute of Group Analysis and am an active student member of the Group Analytic Society International. I have a valid DBS, ICO registration, HCPC registration, BAAT membership and the appropriate insurance for online and face to face therapy.
