ABOUT

We do not need to be isolated in our isolation

Access Art Therapy has been set up in response to COVID-19, by Aisling Fegan, a HCPC registered art psychotherapist and Croydon resident. This grassroots service offers accessible art therapy for people living in our community and safe reflective spaces for those working on the frontline. It is a community-led, clinical art psychotherapy service, underpinned with psychodynamic and group analytic theories.

Frontline workers and their families will be prioritised as much as possible. Individual clinical supervision and reflective practice groups are available in the evenings and early mornings. Sessions can be particularly useful for people who are experiencing high levels of stress, trauma or at risk of burnout. These confidential spaces can help people to safely process and reflect on the emotional impact of your work. Sessions are available online and face to face when it is possible.

Evening art therapy sessions are available for parents, guardians and carers of all ages. Aiming to safely support people as they process the affects of this terrible crisis, sessions offer a dynamic holding environment and an embodied sense of community cohesion. Groups for children will be available, depending on need. This aspect of your care is networked with other services as needed, to ensure safe and professional practice that is not happening in isolation. Self-referrals and referrals from families, health and social care professionals are accepted.

An initial consultation is required, to learn more about your needs and think together about how these sessions might be for you and/or your child. For more information, please contact Aisling (also known as Ash) on 07527156037.  

ART THERAPY

Art therapy, also known as art psychotherapy is a type of psychological therapy as opposed to an art class or hobby. Art therapy can enable the safe expression of distressing thoughts and upsetting feelings. Using art media as its primary form of communication within a therapeutic relationship, the inherently, visceral processes held within the context of art therapy can help people to find meaning on a deeper level.

Sometimes words are not enough. With a focus in the here and now, art therapy can be particularly useful for people who are experiencing mental distress, trauma, loss and displacement. It allows for an experience of being contained and help those who are suffering to reinstate and regulate responses. You do not need to have any skill to make use of art therapy, making it accessible to people of all ages and abilities. 

Individual sessions are 50 minutes long and groups tend to be between 1 hour and 90 minutes. Informed consent must be provided prior to joining art therapy sessions. The artwork and content of art therapy sessions is confidential.

INFORMATION FOR COMMUNITY MEMBERS
Cost will not be a barrier to you accessing art therapy.
This is a 'pay what you can' service. Therapy rates will be discussed as part of your initial consultation. Some people may feel in a position to self-fund their art therapy, others will be able to access sessions free of charge or at reduced rates, depending on their circumstances. Our community is in this together.

AISLING FEGAN

Known as Ash, I am a HCPC registered art psychotherapist, clinical supervisor and Croydon resident. Working across a broad range of issues including community crisis, bereavement, trauma, anxiety, displacement, dementia and domestic violence, I am a specialist in the safe, delivery of community outreach, art psychotherapy.

Between June 2017 and April 2020, I worked as a Clinical Lead in the immediate aftermath and crisis response to the Grenfell Tower fire. Working with all ages, I have over 10 years of experience within schools, charities, the NHS, private domiciliary care agencies and community centres. I am also an experienced service and operations manager.

My approach is informed by psychodynamic and group analytic theories. I have a Masters in art psychotherapy, qualifying with distinctions from Goldsmiths University in 2015. Undertaking a Foundation in Group Analysis with the Institute of Group Analysis in London, I am a student member of the Group Analytic Society International; holding a valid DBS, ICO registration and the appropriate insurance for online and face to face therapy. I have continued learning in order to ensure the safe delivery of online therapy.
