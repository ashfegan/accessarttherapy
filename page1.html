About Aisling

Pronouns: she / her


Aisling Fegan is an Irish artist, HCPC registered art psychotherapist and group analytic therapist. 

Based in Newark-on-Trent (UK) and working online, Aisling offers individual and group psychotherapy for adults living locally & globally. Outreach art therapy is available in Newark-on-Trent for people living with dementia.


Being a lived experience practitioner, Aisling does not see people through a medicalised lens and knows how politics permeates us deeply to our core. Aisling has over 10 years of community-based work experience. She proudly works for icap; an organisation that supports members of the Irish community in Britain.  


Individual Psychotherapy

Psychotherapy is a form of a psychological therapy; underpinned with psychodynamic and psychoanalytic thinking. Originating with Sigmund Freud, psychotherapy offers an opportunity to make sense of how feelings and behaviours in the present are shaped by the past. We work together within a process, full of depth and meaning to make sense of underlying processes that may be causing psychic tension or distress. .

Finding the right therapist is key. It is important that you work with a psychotherapist who you feel comfortable with, who you can feel you can speak openly with and who works at your pace. This isn't a quick fix. This type of therapy requires time, curiosity and commitment.

"Our work together is led by you. You can express your thoughts and feelings in whatever way you feel most comfortable - with words, art making or silence. From this, we will create a language and way of understanding." Aisling Fegan, August 2022.


What is group analytic therapy?

Group analytic therapy uses the group as the primary mechanism for change. According to founder Dr S.H. Foulkes, “the method and theory of group analysis is concerned with a dynamic understanding of the inner working of the human mind as a social, multi-personal phenomenon.” (Foulkes 1975) 

Group work is essentially about authenticity, human connection and lasting, meaningful change. Dynamics arising in group therapy speak to our wider social, cultural and political context. To echo the phrase “the personal is political” (a quote that arose during the student movement in the late 1960’s), the personal is understood and truly appreciated as political in group analytic therapy. This can be a reparative and deeply healing experience for group members. 

Group therapy offers us an opportunity to think beyond our social, cultural and political norms. This is something one of the founding members of the Institute of Group Analysis, Patrick De Maré referred to as outsight; “the outward expansion of social consciousness and thoughtfulness”. (Lenn & Stefano, 2012, p. 129) 

Online Therapy


Online therapy offers accessibility for people who ordinarily would not be able to attend therapy in-person. Maybe you live in a remote part of the world or have commitments like caring for an elderly relative, parenting, your hours of employment or education? Or perhaps, online therapy suits your health needs. Maybe, you travel a lot and are looking for a consistent space in your life; group therapy can be an anchor.


Online therapy gives us the ability to cross borders. Being in group therapy with others from around the world can help us to feel more in touch with our global reality. It is an opportunity to see and think, around and beyond nationality. This process can give a completely different perspective on where we find ourselves. It can redefine what our ‘real’ is. For some people, this can be a life line.

 

What is art therapy?

Art therapy, also known as art psychotherapy, is a type of psychological therapy as opposed to an art class or hobby. It offers a safe way to communicate and contain thoughts and feelings, too difficult to express with words alone. Sometimes, there are no words.

It is suitable for people who are suffering because of low mood, isolation, worry, traumatic life events, eating problems, difficult social & cultural environments, loss or loneliness. Art therapy is for all ages. You do not need to have any particular skill in art to make use of the art therapy. You do not even have to make art in sessions! Any artwork made is confidential.
