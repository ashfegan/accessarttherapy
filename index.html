About me

Aisling Fegan

Pronouns: she / her
Gaelic Irish name pronounced: Ash-ling


I'm an Irish artist and HCPC registered art psychotherapist based in Newark-on-Trent. I offer community orientated, art therapy locally and internationally, online. 

I am lived experienced practitioner so I do not see people through a medicalised lens. Politics, our environment, cultural context and social justice matters; especially when we are thinking about our minds and the ways we suffer.

I work with a wide range of issues including the climate crisis, depression, anxiety, addiction, social injustice, abuse, diasporic related issues, work stress, C/PTSD, trauma, GSRD (Gender, Sex & Relationship Diversity), eating problems, race and class issues.

Alongside private practice, I proudly work for icap; an organisation that supports our Irish community in Britain. I am also establishing a community art therapy service as part of a new, artist collective in Newark - launching 2024.

Graduating from Goldsmiths University of London (2015), I have a masters in art psychotherapy, a degree in modelmaking and design for film and theatre from Ireland’s National Film School (2007) and a diploma in group analytic psychotherapy with Group Analysis India (2022).

What is art therapy?

"Art therapy is a form of psychotherapy that uses visual and tactile media as a means of self expression and communication. Art therapists aim to support people of all ages and abilities and at all stages of life, to discover an outlet for often complex and confusing feelings, and foster self awareness and growth." (BAAT, 2024)

Art therapists are trained and experienced in using art and creativity combined with psychotherapy models to help people to express complex feelings in a safe way. It is suitable for people who have experienced trauma, domestic and political violence, injustice, displacement, abuse, low mood, eating problems, isolation, worry, grief and loneliness.

You do not need to have any particular skill in art to make use of the art therapy. You do not even have to make art in sessions! Art therapy is not dependant on spoken language so it is suitable for all ages. Any artwork made is confidential.

As social action, art therapy can offer community-led instruction and increase sensitivity in people who are indifferent. It can create change, elevate awareness, deepen insight and it can help us to understand the origins of problems. It's affects are far reaching, long lasting and sustainable. 

Groups

Groups are a microcosm of our wider society. So, dynamics arising in group therapy echo our personal, social, cultural and political context.

Group therapy can help us to make sense of how the past influences the present. They encourage imagination, connection and healthy relationships, improve communication skills and reduce feelings of isolation. Groups can help people to find their authentic voice and can be a life line for people who are isolated; especially for those who are suffering because of social or cultural circumstances.

I offer long-term, art therapy art therapy groups; underpinned using psychodynamic and group analytic thinking. There is a maximum of eight people in the group; meeting once, sometimes twice a week for 90 minutes; either online or in Newark-on-Trent.

Fees

Our first 30-minute conversation is free of charge.

Typically, individual art therapy costs between £50 and £80 per session and group art therapy costs between £100 and £160 per month. The cost will vary depending on what you can afford.

Spaces reserved for people with lower incomes. Fee reduction available on request. No one is turned away due to lack of funds.
