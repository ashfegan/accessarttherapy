What is art therapy?

- It's empowerment.


Art therapy is a safe way to express thoughts and feelings. It can create change, elevate awareness, deepen insight and it can help us to understand the origins of problems. Art therapy as a form of social action, can offer community-led instruction and increase sensitivity in people who are indifferent. It's affects are far reaching, long lasting and sustainable.

Art therapists are trained and experienced in using art and creativity combined with psychotherapy models to help people to express complex feelings in a safe way. It suitable for people who have experienced trauma, domestic and political violence, injustice, displacement, abuse, low mood, eating problems, isolation, worry, grief and loneliness.

You do not need to have any particular skill in art to make use of the art therapy. You do not even have to make art in sessions! It is not dependant on spoken language so it is suitable for all ages. Any artwork made is confidential.

Groups


Group therapy can help us to make sense of how the past influences the present. They encourage imagination, connection and healthy relationships, improve communication skills and reduce feelings of isolation. Groups can help people to find their authentic voice. 

I offer long-term, art therapy groups; underpinned using psychodynamic and group analytic thinking. There is a maximum of eight people in the group; meeting once, sometimes twice a week for 90 minutes; either online or in Newark-on-Trent.  

How does group therapy work?

Groups are a microcosm of our wider society. So, dynamics arising in group therapy echo our wider social, cultural and political context. Group can be a life line for people who are isolated; especially for those who are suffering because of social or cultural circumstances. This is something one of the founding members of the Institute of Group Analysis, Patrick De Maré referred to as outsight; “the outward expansion of social consciousness and thoughtfulness”. (Lenn & Stefano, 2012, p. 129) 

"So, we can still only imagine the potential of a group of five hundred, seated in tiered concentric circles, meeting at least weekly over a period of, say, five years. Imagine, if in a group of that size,everyone could have a voice?"
Dick Blackwell, 2012.

About

Aisling Fegan

Pronouns: she / her
Irish Gaelic name pronounced: Ash-ling.

I'm an Irish artist and community art therapist. I work with people in Newark-on-Trent, online and I also proudly work for icap; an organisation that supports members of the Irish community in Britain.

Politics, our environment, cultural context and social justice matters; especially when we are thinking about how minds suffer. I am lived experienced practitioner so I have first-hand experience of this. Humanity always comes first.

Graduating from Goldsmiths University of London in 2015, I have a masters in art psychotherapy, a degree in modelmaking and design for film and theatre from Ireland’s National Film School (2007) and a diploma in group analytic psychotherapy with Group Analysis India (2022).

Fees

Our first 30-minute conversation is free of charge.

The cost of therapy will vary depending on what you can afford. Usually, individual art therapy costs between £50 and £80 per session and group art therapy costs £100 per month.

Spaces are reserved for people with lower incomes, students and people living in the Global South. No one will be turned away due to lack of funds.
