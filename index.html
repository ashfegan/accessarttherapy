Online Therapy

Online therapy offers accessibility and the possibility of meaningful connection. Maybe you live in a remote place and want to think with other curious people, outside of your immediate bubble? Or, maybe you have commitments in your life like caring for a relative, parenting, your hours of employment or education? Perhaps, online therapy suits your health needs? Or maybe, you travel a lot? Online therapy can be a consistent and safe space in your life. It can be an anchor.

Being in online group therapy with others from around the world can help us to see and think outside of our social norms. This simply is not possible with in-person therapy. 

To talk about whether online therapy is right for you, get in touch. 

Psychotherapy

Psychotherapy is a form of a psychological therapy; underpinned with psychodynamic and psychoanalytic thinking. Originating with Sigmund Freud, psychotherapy offers an opportunity to make sense of how feelings and behaviours in the present are shaped by the past. We work together within a process, full of depth and meaning. Psychotherapy can help to uncover the root causes of psychic tension or distress.

Psychotherapy with lasting and meaningful change is not a quick fix. It requires time, authenticity, curiosity and commitment. Finding the right therapist is key. It is important that you work with a psychotherapist who you feel comfortable with and who works at your pace.


"As an art psychotherapist, I work with both words and art in psychotherapy. So, you can express your thoughts and feelings in whatever way you feel most comfortable - by talking or making art. Silence is ok too. From this, we will create a language together and a way of understanding." Aisling Fegan, August 2023. 

Group Psychotherapy

Group psychotherapy is a 90-minute, weekly therapy group; hosted online with other adults who are committed to the group and personal development. There is a maximum of eight people; meeting at the same time very week; long term. It is a place where you can share your experiences, thoughts and feelings at your own pace. The group will be there for you - week in, week out; alongside your life events, struggles and joys.

Group psychotherapy can help you to find your voice, express difficult thoughts and feeling, establish and maintain healthy relationships, improve communication skills, reduce feelings of isolation and make sense of how the past influences the present.


How does it work?

Groups are a microcosm of our wider society. So, dynamics arising in group therapy echo our wider social, cultural and political context. Group psychotherapy offers an opportunity to think beyond our social norms. This is something one of the founding members of the Institute of Group Analysis, Patrick De Maré referred to as outsight; “the outward expansion of social consciousness and thoughtfulness”. (Lenn & Stefano, 2012, p. 129) This can be a life line for people who are suffering because of social or cultural circumstances.

...a little bit about the history of group analysis.

In 1940, psychiatrist and refugee (who escaped Nazi Germany) Dr S.H. Foulkes invited the people sitting in his full waiting room to free associate with each other. From this, he brought these people together to undertake therapy as a group. And like this, group analytic therapy was born.

Later at Northfield’s military hospital, Foulkes worked alongside John Rickman and Wilfred Bion in response to World War Two. Here, he continued to develop his group analytic thinking and with others, pioneered a group programme to serve the needs of men who had experienced terrible things during war. 

Art Therapy

Art therapy, also known as art psychotherapy, is a type of psychological therapy as opposed to an art class or hobby. Thoughts and feelings can be difficult to put into words. Sometimes, there are no words. Art therapy offers a creative way to explore and express emotions.

You do not need to have any particular skill in art to make use of the art therapy. So, art therapy is for all ages. You do not even have to make art in sessions! Any artwork made is confidential. Art therapy is suitable for people who are suffering because of traumatic life events, eating problems, difficult social & cultural environments, low mood, isolation, worry, loss or loneliness.
