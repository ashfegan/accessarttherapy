What is Art Therapy?

Art therapy is a form of psychological therapy that uses creative practices like drawing, painting and sculpture to express difficult thoughts and feelings in safe way. It can enable people to process the impact of trauma, build trust, feel less isolated, develop healthy relationships and grow confidence.

As social action, art therapy offers an accessible and wonderful way for people to find their voices and magnify them - if they want to. Art therapy can create change, elevate awareness, deepen insight. It can help us to understand the origins of problems. It's affects are far reaching, long lasting and sustainable.

Art therapists are trained and experienced in using art and creativity combined with psychotherapy models to help people to express complex feelings in a safe way. It is suitable for people who have experienced trauma, domestic and political violence, injustice, displacement, abuse, low mood, eating problems, isolation, worry, grief and loneliness.

You do not need to have any particular skill in art to make use of the art therapy. You do not even have to make art in sessions! Art therapy is not dependant on spoken language so it is suitable for all ages. Any artwork made is confidential.

Group Therapy

Groups are a microcosm of our wider society. So, dynamics arising in group therapy echo our personal, social, cultural and political context.

Group therapy can help us to make sense of how the past influences the present. They encourage imagination, connection and healthy relationships, improve communication skills and reduce feelings of isolation. Groups can help people to find their authentic voice and can be a life line for people who are isolated; especially for those who are suffering because of social or cultural circumstances.

I offer long-term, art therapy art therapy groups; underpinned using psychodynamic and group analytic thinking. There is a maximum of eight people in the group; meeting once, sometimes twice a week for 90 minutes; either online or in Newark-on-Trent.

About me

Aisling Fegan
Pronouns: she / her
Gaelic Irish name pronounced: Ash-ling

I am a HCPC registered art psychotherapist and resident of Newark-on-Trent with a passion for meaningful, community-led development. I work locally in Newark and internationally, online.

Alongside my art therapy practice based at Coreset, I proudly work for icap; an organisation that supports the Irish community in Britain. I am experienced in incident response, community crisis and disaster recovery, and have worked as an art psychotherapist within the tv and film industry.

I warmly welcome neurodivergence, queer and questioning folx, those exploring alternative lifestyles, and those who place social and environmental justice at the centre of their lives. I have extensive experience with adults and young people with a range of issues. Such as, eating disorders, trauma, violence (domestic, social and political), childhood sexual abuse, survivors of institutional abuse including 'Mother and Baby Homes' in Ireland and the UK, addictions, depression, anxiety, C/PTSD and diasporic experiences.

Graduating from Goldsmiths University of London in 2015 with a masters in art psychotherapy, I have a degree in modelmaking and design for film and theatre from Ireland’s National Film School (2007) and a diploma in group analytic psychotherapy with Group Analysis India (2022).

I am a dual experienced, art therapist; with lived experience of acute mental illness and neurodivergence. So, I know that our environment matters when we are thinking about how our minds can suffer. In my work as an art therapist, I centre people’s human experiences in the context of political, cultural and social positionality. This process is aided and often led by art making practices.
