ART THERAPY GROUP

Monday 9.30 - 11am (BST)

This is a psychodynamic, art psychotherapy group for adults; hosted from Newark on Trent in the East Midlands, UK. The group is open to people who are local to Newark and people who live across the globe. Everyone is welcome!

Art therapy, also known art psychotherapy offers a way to communicate and contain thoughts and feelings that are too difficult to express with words alone. Often, there are not enough words to fully express what we think or how we feel. Sometimes there simply are no words

Art therapy groups offer space to connect, share and think creatively with others. There are no set themes. You do not need to have any skills in art to make use of art therapy. The content of your therapy, including any artwork created, is strictly confidential. You do not have to make art.

Online art therapy sessions are suitable for adults who are suffering because of distress, difficulties in social environments, low mood, worry, traumatic life events, eating difficulties, loss or exhaustion.

There will be a maximum of eight regular group members.
The group will run for 90 minutes, weekly during term time for a minimum of two years. So, you can feel secure in the knowledge that our group is here for you long term.

You can join us from the comfort of your own home. All you need to a quiet and private room with an internet connection and art materials of your choice. 

This group costs £140 per month; for 11 months. (No payment in August). Talk to Aisling about money if the cost is too high.

GROUP PSYCHOTHERAPY

Wedensdays 9.15 - 10.45am (BST)

This is group analytic, psychotherapy group for adults; hosted from Newark on Trent in the East Midlands, UK. It is a talking therapy group. The group is open to people who are local to Newark and people who live across the globe. Everyone is welcome!

You can join us from the comfort of your own home. All you need to a quiet and private room with an internet connection.

There will be a maximum of eight regular group members.
The group will run for 90 minutes, weekly during term time for a minimum of two years. So, you can feel secure in the knowledge that our group is here for you long term.

This therapy group is a place to think with others and express thoughts and feelings about personal, social, cultural and political issues. It is suitable for therapy students and those training in health and social care professions.

The group it conducted by Aisling (Ash-ling) Fegan; an Irish artist, art therapist, group psychotherapist and group analyst in training. Each group member will meet with Aisling several times, before joining the group. This will be an opportunity to think about your needs, talk about the group and ask questions.

This group costs £140 per month; for 11 months. (No payment in August). Talk to Aisling about money if the cost is too high.

ABOUT 

Aisling Fegan (she / her)

I am an Irish artist, community art therapist, group psychotherapist and group analyst in training with Group Analysis India. I with a lived experience of wellness owed to a therapeutic community, So, I do not look at other humans through a medicalised lens. 

My thoughts are influenced by group analytic, psychodynamic and trauma informed thinking. I also have a degree in modelmaking and design for film and theatre. I lived in London for over 10 years before moving to the East Midlands and I have an understanding of life and history in the Irish counties of Meath, Cavan and Wicklow.

I am proudly working for icap; an organisation that works with members of our Irish diaphora in the UK and on island of Ireland.  In June 2017, I worked as part of the community-led response to the Grenfell Tower fire and helped to create Latimer Community Art Therapy. With Solas Mind, I provided art psychotherapy to people working in film production, through the pandemic. Other places I have worked include Action for Refugees in Lewisham, Swiss Cottage School, SweetTree Homecare Services, the Bray Lakers (Co. Wicklow, Ireland), Cranstoun’s Trelawn House, South West London and St Georges NHS Trust, Age UK, Kensington and Chelsea Memory Service (NHS), Schoen Clinic Chelsea, and Studio Upstairs. Experienced in clinical and operational leadership, I am also a former Senior Dementia Advisor for the Royal Borough of Kensington and Chelsea.  


Qualifications:
IGA International Diploma in Group Analysis from the IGA (UK), Group Analysis India (Aug 2021 – July 2022)

Certificate: An introduction to working with people with eating disorders and body dysmorphia, Tavistock and Portman NHS Foundation, London (Oct 2020 – June 2021)

Foundation Certificate in Group Analysis, Institute of Group Analysis, London (Oct 2019 – July 2020)

Masters in Art Psychotherapy (with Distinctions), Goldsmiths, University of London (Sept 2012 – June 2015)

Degree in Model Making and Design for Film and Media (Pass with Merit), Institute of Art, Design and Technology Ireland (Sept 2004 – May 2007)


I have a valid DBS, ICO registration, HCPC registration (AS15235), associate membership with the Institute of Group Analysis and the Group Analytic Society International, BAAT membership and the appropriate insurance for online and face-to-face therapy.
