What is art therapy?

- It's empowerment.

Art therapy is a safe way to express thoughts and feelings. It works to aid expression, create change, elevate awareness, deepen insight and understand the origins of problems. In socially oriented action, art therapy can offer community-led instruction and increase sensitivity to the social context of human suffering. It's affects are far reaching and sustainable.

Art therapists are trained and experienced in using art and creativity combined with psychotherapy models to help people to express complex feelings in a safe way. It suitable for people who have experienced trauma, domestic and political violence, injustice, displacement, abuse, low mood, eating problems, isolation, worry, grief and loneliness.

You do not need to have any particular skill in art to make use of the art therapy. You do not even have to make art in sessions! It is not dependant on spoken language so it is suitable for all ages.
Any artwork made is confidential.

Group Therapy

Group therapy make sense of how the past influences the present, encourages and maintain healthy relationships, improves communication skills and reduces feelings of isolation. It can help people to find their authentic voice. 

I offer long-term, art therapy groups; underpinned using psychodynamic and group analytic thinking. There is a maximum of eight people in the group; meeting once, sometimes twice a week for 90 minutes; either online or in Newark-on-Trent.  


How does it work?
Groups are a microcosm of our wider society. So, dynamics arising in group therapy echo our wider social, cultural and political context. To see outside of your situation - can help to expand awareness and deepen insight. It be a life line; especially for people who are suffering because of social or cultural circumstances. This is something one of the founding members of the Institute of Group Analysis, Patrick De Maré referred to as outsight; “the outward expansion of social consciousness and thoughtfulness”. (Lenn & Stefano, 2012, p. 129)

Fees

Our first 30-minute conversation is free of charge.

The cost of therapy will vary depending on what you can afford.

Usually, individual art therapy costs between £50 and £80 per session and group art therapy costs £100 per month.

Spaces are reserved for people with lower incomes, students and people living in the Global South. No one will be turned away due to lack of funds. 


Specialist rates for organisations are available. Community Outreach also available.
