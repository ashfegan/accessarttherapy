What is Art Therapy?

Art therapy is a form of psychological therapy that uses creative practices like drawing, painting and sculpture to express thoughts and feelings. It can enable people to process the impact of trauma, build trust, feel less isolated, develop healthy relationships and grow confidence.

As social action, art therapy offers an accessible and collective way for individuals and communities to find their voices and magnify them. Art therapy can create change, elevate awareness, deepen insight in our community. It can help us to understand the origins of problems. It's affects are far reaching, long lasting and sustainable.

Art Therapy is suitable for people who have experienced trauma, domestic and political violence, injustice, displacement, abuse, low mood, eating problems, isolation, worry, grief and loneliness. Art therapists are trained and experienced in using art and creativity combined with psychotherapy models to help people to express complex feelings in a safe way.

You do not need to have any particular skill in art to make use of the art therapy. You do not even have to make art in sessions! Art therapy is not dependant on spoken language so it is suitable for all ages. Any artwork made is confidential.

Why Group Therapy?

Groups are a microcosm of our wider society. Dynamics arising in group therapy echo our personal, social, cultural and political context. And so, group therapy offers space to explore ourselves and the world around us.

Group therapy can help us to make sense of how the past influences the present. They encourage imagination, connection and healthy relationships, improve communication skills and reduce feelings of isolation. Groups can help people to find their authentic voice and can be a life line for people who are isolated; especially for those who are suffering because of social or cultural circumstances.

I offer long-term, art therapy groups for people living locally in Newark-on-Trent and internationally. There is a maximum of eight people in the group; meeting once, sometimes twice a week for 90 minutes; either online or in Newark-on-Trent.

About me

Aisling Fegan
Pronouns: she / her
Gaelic Irish name pronounced: Ash-ling

I am an Irish artist, HCPC registered art psychotherapist and resident of Newark-on-Trent with a passion for meaningful, community-led development. I work locally in Newark and internationally, online.

The art therapy that I offer focuses creativity, self-expression and the world around us - rather than diagnosis. I am a lived experienced practitioner (LXP) and my work is influenced by therapeutic community ethos and group analytic thinking. I am experienced in community incident response and have worked as an art psychotherapist within the tv and film industry. 

In Newark, I am part of Coreset; a new artist-led collective and offer studio based, art therapy and reflective practice. I proudly work for icap; an organisation that supports the Irish community in Britain.
  
I warmly welcome neurodivergence, queer and questioning folx, those exploring alternative lifestyles, and those who place social and environmental justice at the centre of their lives. I have extensive experience working with a range of issues including eating disorders, trauma, survivors of childhood sexual abuse and institutional abuse including 'Mother and Baby Homes' in Ireland and the UK, violence (domestic, social and political), addictions, depression, anxiety and diasporic experiences.

Graduating from Goldsmiths University of London in 2015 with a masters in art psychotherapy, I have a degree in modelmaking and design for film and theatre from Ireland’s National Film School (2007) and a diploma in group analytic psychotherapy with Group Analysis India (2022).
