Online Therapy

At it's core, online therapy (with Aisling) is about realism and meaningful, cross border connection. It offers space to think about personal and shared social issues; like racism, the climate crisis and eco-anxiety.

Online therapy can be individual or in a group. Being in group therapy with others from around the world can help us to think outside of our own heads, our culture and society. This simply is not possible with in-person therapy. This process offers an authentic and alternative perspective. This can be a life line.

For many people, online therapy is more accessible and the preferred experience. Maybe you live in a remote place and want to think with other curious people, outside of your immediate bubble? Or, maybe you have commitments in your life like caring for a relative, parenting, your hours of employment or education? Perhaps, online therapy suits your health needs? Or maybe, you travel a lot? Online therapy can be a consistent and safe space in your life. It can be an anchor.

To talk about whether online therapy is right for you, get in touch. 

Psychotherapy

Psychotherapy is a form of a psychological therapy; underpinned with psychodynamic and psychoanalytic thinking. Originating with Sigmund Freud, psychotherapy offers an opportunity to make sense of how feelings and behaviours in the present are shaped by the past. We work together within a process, full of depth and meaning. Psychotherapy can help to uncover the root causes of psychic tension or distress.

Finding the right therapist is key. It is important that you work with a psychotherapist who you feel comfortable with and who works at your pace. Therapy with lasting and meaningful change is not a quick fix. It requires time, authenticity, curiosity and commitment.

"As an art psychotherapist, I work with both words and art in therapy. So, you can express your thoughts and feelings in whatever way you feel most comfortable - by talking or making art. Silence is ok too. From this, we will create a language together. A way of understanding." Aisling Fegan, August 2023. 


What is Group Analytic Therapy?

Group analytic therapy uses the group as the primary mechanism for change. According to founder Dr S.H. Foulkes, “the method and theory of group analysis is concerned with a dynamic understanding of the inner working of the human mind as a social, multi-personal phenomenon.” (Foulkes 1975)

Dynamics arising in group therapy speak to our wider social, cultural and political context. To echo the phrase “the personal is political” (a quote that arose during the student movement in the late 1960’s), the personal is understood and truly appreciated as political in group analytic therapy. This can be a reparative and deeply healing experience for group members.

Group therapy offers us an opportunity to think beyond our social, cultural and political norms. This is something one of the founding members of the Institute of Group Analysis, Patrick De Maré referred to as outsight; “the outward expansion of social consciousness and thoughtfulness”. (Lenn & Stefano, 2012, p. 129)

...a little bit about the history of group analysis.

In 1940, psychiatrist and refugee (who escaped Nazi Germany) Dr S.H. Foulkes invited the people sitting in his full waiting room to free associate with each other. From this, he brought these people together to undertake therapy as a group. And like this, group analytic therapy was born.

Later at Northfield’s military hospital, Foulkes worked alongside John Rickman and Wilfred Bion in response to World War Two. Here, he continued to develop his group analytic thinking and with others, pioneered a group programme to serve the needs of men who had experienced terrible things during war. 

What is Art Therapy?

Art therapy, also known as art psychotherapy, is a type of psychological therapy as opposed to an art class or hobby. Art therapy uses the art making process as the primary mode of communication. We centre the art and the art making process. It offers a safe way to communicate and contain thoughts and feelings, too difficult to express with words alone. Sometimes, there are no words and that's ok too.

Art therapy is for all ages. You do not need to have any particular skill in art to make use of the art therapy. You do not even have to make art in sessions! Any artwork made is confidential. Art therapy is suitable for people who are suffering because of traumatic life events, eating problems, difficult social & cultural environments, low mood, isolation, worry, loss or loneliness.
